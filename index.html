VoteGS: Large-Scale 3D Gaussian Splatting Pruning via Neighbor Voting

Yong Kou1, Zeng Fan1, Xia Yuan1, 2, Haobo Liang1, Yixian Li1, Haoran He1, Yanci Zhang1, *

1College of Computer Science, Sichuan University, Chengdu 610065, China
2Chengdu University of Information Technology, Chengdu 610225, China

Abstract

Recent pruning methods for 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) focus on eliminating redundant Gaussians to enhance efficiency, but suffer from issues such as neglected spatial dependencies, limited large-scale scene support, and poor generalization. To address these, we propose VoteGS, a novel pruning framework for large-scale 3DGS. (1) We design a neighbor voting mechanism and Gaussian merging process. The former computes each Gaussian’s existence probability by integrating spatial distance, rendering contribution, temporal decay, multi-view visibility, and spherical harmonics strength, while the latter merges low-probability Gaussians based on similarity to enhance rendering quality. (2) We employ multi-GPU parallelism for large-scale scenes and cross-GPU probability synchronization to address incomplete probability estimates for boundary Gaussians in distributed training. (3) Our method adopts PSNR-based feedback to adaptively adjust pruning intensity, ensuring stable pruning effects in diverse scenes. Extensive experiments show that VoteGS achieves up to 5× compression in model size and up to 60% reduction in GPU memory during training while maintaining rendering quality, regardless of scene scale.
